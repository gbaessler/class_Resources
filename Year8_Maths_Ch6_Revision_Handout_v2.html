Simple Familiar

Ratios — Simplifying, Equivalent & Sharing

🎯Q1–Q3 and Q5 types. Uses your Chapter 3 HCF skill! A ratio works exactly like a fraction — simplify by dividing both (or all) parts by the HCF.
Worked Example 1 — Simplifying with Different Units
Simplify 600 g to 3 kg
1
Convert to same units: 3 kg = 3000 g
2
Write ratio: 600 : 3000
3
HCF = 600 → divide both: 600÷600 : 3000÷600
✓ 1 : 5
👀 Same method as simplifying a fraction using HCF — Chapter 3 skill!
Worked Example 2 — Sharing in a 3-Part Ratio
Three friends share $720 in the ratio 1 : 3 : 5. How much does each person receive?
1
Add parts: 1 + 3 + 5 = 9 parts
2
Value of 1 part: $720 ÷ 9 = $80
3
Person A: 1 × $80 = $80  |  B: 3 × $80 = $240  |  C: 5 × $80 = $400
✓
Check: $80 + $240 + $400 = $720 ✓
✓ $80 · $240 · $400
💡 Check: Always add your answers back up — they MUST equal the original total. If they don't, you've made an error in step 2.

Scale Drawings & Maps

🎯Q6, Q7, Q11 and Q13 types. Scale 1:n means every 1 cm on the drawing = n cm in real life. Q11 requires a ruler — get one ready before perusal!
Worked Example 3 — Map Scale to Actual Distance
Scale 1:20 000. Map distance = 9 cm. Find actual distance in metres.
1
Actual cm: 9 × 20 000 = 180 000 cm
2
Convert to m: 180 000 ÷ 100 = 1800 m
✓ 1800 m
Worked Example 4 — Finding a Scale Factor
A 5 cm measurement on a drawing represents 35 m actual. Find the scale factor.
1
Convert actual to cm: 35 m = 3500 cm
2
Write ratio: 5 : 3500 → divide by 5: 1 : 700
✓ Scale 1 : 700
⚠ Q11 and Q13 need a ruler! During perusal, find these questions and get your ruler ready. Measure carefully in cm, record your measurement in your working, then multiply by the scale.
Practice — Scale Try Without Looking!

Scale 1:5000. Map distance = 7 cm. Find actual distance in metres.
7×5000=35000cm ÷100 = 350 m
P8 SF
3 cm on a drawing = 45 m actual. Find scale factor.
45m=4500cm; 3:4500 ÷3 = 1:1500
P9 CF

8.4×250000=2100000cm=21000m=21 km
Simple Familiar

Rates — Expressing in Simplest Form

🎯Q9 type. A rate compares two quantities with different units. Always include the units in your answer — 70 without "km/h" only earns half marks!
Worked Example 5 — Express as a Rate
a) A cyclist rides 240 km in 4 hours    b) 450 sheep on 150 hectares
a
240 ÷ 4 = 60 → 60 km/h
b
450 ÷ 150 = 3 → 3 sheep/ha
✓ a) 60 km/h  |  b) 3 sheep/ha
💡 "Per" = ÷. "km per hour" means km ÷ hours. Always divide the top quantity by the bottom quantity to get a "per 1" rate.
Practice — Rates Try Without Looking!
P10 SF
420 litres flows through a pipe in 6 minutes. Express as a rate.
420÷6=70 L/min
P11 SF
A factory produces 800 items in 5 hours. Express as a rate.
800÷5=160 items/hour
P12 SF
750 cattle on 500 hectares. Express as a rate in simplest form.
750÷500=1.5 cattle/ha
SF / CU

Speed, Distance & Time — DST Triangle

🎯Q10 and Q13 types. Always convert time to hours before using the km/h formula. Q13 is the most complex question — it combines scale reading with DST in 4 steps.
Worked Example 6 — Average Speed (Q10 Type)
A bus travels 390 km in 260 minutes. Find average speed in km/h.
1
Convert minutes to hours: 260 ÷ 60 = 4⅓ hours
2
S = D ÷ T: 390 ÷ (13/3) = 390 × 3/13 = 90 km/h
✓ 90 km/h
Worked Example 7 — Map + DST Combined (Q13 Type — 4 Steps)
Map scale 1:100 000. Measured map distance = 7 cm. Speed limit = 80 km/h. How long does the journey take in minutes?
1
Measure & scale: 7 × 100 000 = 700 000 cm
2
Convert to km: 700 000 ÷ 100 000 = 7 km
3
Apply T = D ÷ S: 7 ÷ 80 = 0.0875 hours
4
Convert to minutes: 0.0875 × 60 = 5.25 minutes
✏
Sentence: "It will take approximately 5.25 minutes to complete the journey."
✓ 5.25 minutes — with sentence answer
⚠ Q13 sentence answer is worth a mark! Even if your calculation is correct, you lose a reasoning mark without a complete sentence. Practice writing: "It will take approximately ___ minutes to drive from ___ to ___."
Practice — DST Try Without Looking!
P13 SF
A train travels 540 km in 360 minutes. Find average speed in km/h.
360min=6hrs; S=540÷6=90 km/h
P14 SF
A car drives at 100 km/h for 3.5 hours. How far does it travel?
D=S×T=100×3.5=350 km
P15 CU
Map scale 1:180 000. Distance on map = 6 cm. Speed = 90 km/h. Journey time in minutes?
6×180000=1080000cm=10.8km; T=10.8÷90=0.12h×60=7.2 min ≈7 min 12 sec
P16 CF
Two partners invest $4000 and $6000. They share profits in ratio of investment. If the smaller partner gets $800, how much does the larger get?
4000:6000=2:3; 1 part=800÷2=$400; larger=3×$400=$1200
CF & CU

Complex Questions — Full Worked Strategy

🎯Q4, Q8, Q12 and Q13 types. Complex Familiar (CF) questions apply familiar methods in new contexts. Complex Unfamiliar (CU) require multi-step reasoning you may not have seen before. Always write a sentence answer for CU questions.
💡 CUBED Strategy for word problems: Circle numbers · Underline the question · Box key words · Equation · Double-check + write sentence
Worked Example 8 — Growth Rate Word Problem (Q12 Type — CU)
A rabbit gained 150 g per month for 8 months. If it weighed 250 g at birth, how much did it weigh at 8 months? Give your answer in kg and write a sentence answer.
1
Growth: 150 × 8 = 1200 g
2
Total: 250 + 1200 = 1450 g
3
Convert: 1450 ÷ 1000 = 1.45 kg
✏
"At 8 months, the rabbit weighed 1.45 kg."
✓ 1.45 kg — with sentence answer
